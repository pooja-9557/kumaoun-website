A Brief Introduction of Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand was the ancient term for the central Indian Himalayas. Its peaks and valleys were known as Svarga loka: a temporary abode of the righteous , and the source of the Ganges. The 27th state of Republic of India, Devbhoomi Uttarakhand that was formed in the year 2000 after being separated from the large state of Uttar Pradesh, is a destination to explore the riches of Indian culture, history, and natural beauty. The state that borders Tibet to the north; Nepal to the east; the state of Uttar Pradesh to the south; and Himachal Pradesh to the west and north-west, is divided into regions, Garhwal and Kumaon, which further break into 13 districts. Earlier, the state was named UttaranchalUttarakhand is known as "the land of the gods" (Devbhumi) becauseof its number of Hindu pilgrimage sites.
"The land of lord" ,actually it's unique because the person who doesn't believe in god admire the presence of lord .The people of Uttarakhand celebrates and perform rituals around the year due to their deep-rooted collection with nature and the rich mythology. Like their simple lifestyle, the festivals and fairs in Uttarakhand are also simple yet culturally rich. Each season is welcome with hearty folk songs and dance and so are the agricultural periods. The ancestor spirit worship is exclusive to the state, Jaagar, as it is locally called, is held to wake the Gods and the local deities up from their inactive stage to solve their problems and shower on them many blessings.

KUMAON

TRADITIONAL DRESS
Women: Traditional dress of uttrakhand is ghagra choli and rangwali pichori. In festivals or any function they mostly wear these traditional dresses with a significant large nose ring (nathuni) and necklace which is known as galoband. The pichora and galoband worn by married women which represent their prosperity. A married women always wear naptial chain (mangalsutra) which represent the culture of Kumaon.
Men: traditional dress of men is kurta pjama with bhoti (jacket).They wear a traditional cap in their functions which represent their sincerity about their culture .

SOURCE OF ECONOMY
Uttarakhand is largely dependent on its agricultureBasmati rice, wheat, soybeans, groundnuts, coarse cereals, pulses, and oil seeds are the most widely grown crops in the state. There are also fruits like apples, oranges, pears, peaches, litchis, and plums that are widely grown in the state and bring a good amount of revenue. There are so many other fruites which aren't konws by other cities like kaffal, farshyan and so many others .

The beautiful and lovely part of uttarkhand is food. there is a lots of food which has unique name like Manduwa ki roti, Dal, Bhaat, Bhang ki chatni, Aalu gutook, Sisunak saag, dubook, Singodi, kumaouni raita etc. The food of uttarakhand is really tasty because they made it by love and affection. Chulan(kitchen), which is made by red soil used to cook the food.

SWEETS
Bal mithai is very popular in Uttarakhand.Bal Mithai is made by cooking khoya (evaporated milk cream) with cane sugar until it becomes dark brown in color, colloquially called 'chocolate' for its color resemblance. This is allowed to settle and cool, and cut into cubes which are then coated with small white balls made of sugar coated roasted poppy seeds.
In uttrakhand singodi mithai is also famous .This sweet made of Khoya (condensed milk) and then wrapped into the leaf of Molu/Roni is iconic. The fresh smell of Molu leaf and the taste of condensed milk flavored with cardamom and coconut will leave you asking for more. Almora is the most famous place to try Singodi in Uttarakhand.
